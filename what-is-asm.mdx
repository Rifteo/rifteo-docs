## What is Attack Surface Management?

Attack Surface Management (ASM) is an ongoing way of finding, tracking, and monitoring everything about your organisation that's visible and reachable from the outside — your websites, servers, subdomains, and other externally exposed systems.

Unlike a one-time audit, ASM isn't a single snapshot. It continuously discovers new assets as they appear (including ones you may not know about, like an old subdomain or a forgotten test server) and keeps monitoring them over time, so you always know what's exposed rather than finding out only when something goes wrong.

This matters because your attack surface changes constantly — new services get spun up, old ones get forgotten, and each one is a potential way in for an attacker. ASM gives you continuous visibility instead of a single point-in-time check.

## Scan Variants

ASM offers three scan variants, each with a different depth of scanning:

| Variant | Depth |
|---------|-------|
| **ASM Soft** | A lighter, faster scan that covers the basics of asset discovery and surface-level checks. Good for frequent, low-overhead monitoring. |
| **ASM Medium** | A more thorough scan that goes deeper into discovered assets, balancing depth and scan time. |
| **ASM Full** | The most comprehensive scan available, digging deepest into every discovered asset. Takes longer to run but surfaces the most detail. |

## How ASM differs from a Pentest

| | ASM | Pentest |
|---|-----|---------|
| **Scope** | Discovers your attack surface automatically — no need to declare specific targets in advance. | Requires declared targets: specific systems or applications agreed on beforehand. |
| **Rules of Engagement** | Automatically defined based on what's discovered — no manual ROE negotiation needed. | Manually agreed upon between your organisation and the testing team before testing begins. |
| **Starting point** | Starts from a client seed (like your domain), then expands outward as new assets are discovered. | Starts from a fixed, pre-agreed list of targets. |
| **Nature** | Continuous and ongoing. | A defined, time-boxed engagement. |
| **Best for** | Ongoing visibility into what's exposed. | In-depth testing of a specific system or application. |

If you're not sure which is right for your organisation, your Rifteo contact can help you decide — many organisations use both, with ASM for continuous monitoring and Pentests for deeper, targeted testing.
