## Overview

Attack Surface Management (ASM) is a way to continuously discover and monitor everything your organisation exposes to the internet — websites, servers, APIs, subdomains — without you having to manually list every system first.

Unlike a traditional audit, where you tell Rifteo exactly what to test, ASM starts from what's publicly visible about your organisation and builds the list of assets for you.

## Why It Matters

Most organisations have more exposed on the internet than they realise — forgotten subdomains, staging environments left open, or services spun up by a different team. Each of these is a potential entry point for an attacker.

ASM gives you:

- **Continuous visibility** — instead of a one-time snapshot, you get an ongoing view of what's exposed.
- **No manual asset list required** — Rifteo discovers assets for you, based on your organisation's public footprint.
- **Early warning** — new or changed exposure is flagged as soon as it's discovered.

## How It Works

You give Rifteo a starting point — typically your company's main domain. From there, Rifteo automatically maps out what's publicly reachable and exposed to the internet, and runs the right checks for what it finds — a web app gets tested differently than a mail server, for example, all without you having to configure anything.

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Your Domain] --> B[Discovery]
    B --> C[Automatic Testing]
    C --> D[Scan Results]
```

- **Your domain** — the starting point Rifteo uses to begin discovery.
- **Discovery** — Rifteo maps out what's publicly reachable from there.
- **Automatic testing** — the right checks are applied depending on what's discovered (a web app, an API, a mail server...).
- **Scan results** — everything discovered and tested is compiled into your results view.

## How ASM Differs from a Pentest

| | **ASM** | **Pentest** |
|---|---|---|
| **Targets** | No targets declared upfront — discovered automatically from your domain | You declare specific targets (assets) before testing starts |
| **Rules of Engagement** | Applied automatically, based on what's discovered | Defined manually by you (forbidden actions, feared events, protections in place) |
| **Starting point** | Your domain | A defined scope you set up step by step |
| **Best for** | Ongoing visibility across everything you expose | A deep, hands-on test of one specific system |
| **Category** | Attack Surface Assessment | Penetration Test |

<Note>
ASM doesn't replace a Pentest — the two are complementary. ASM tells you what's exposed and worth a closer look; a Pentest goes deep on a specific target you've chosen.
</Note>
